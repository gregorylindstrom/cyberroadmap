// Pillar 03 · Training & Labs

Build it with your hands.

Certs prove you studied. Hands-on labs prove you can do the work. Here's the full curated path — from your first reverse shell to production-grade home lab — ordered by what actually builds competence.

Start here Next level Blue team & SOC CTFs YouTube Home lab Python & Linux Python, Data & AI Free courses College pathways

The honest truth about training

Formal education and certifications are the foundation. A degree and Security+ will get you past résumé screening. But that's the floor, not the ceiling.

Building skills at home is required to be successful. Every senior practitioner you admire spent nights and weekends breaking things in their own lab, completing CTFs, walking through IppSec videos at 2x speed. There is no shortcut around this work.

The resources below are the ones that actually build competence. Free where possible, paid only when the jump in quality is worth it, and with clear guidance on the sequence.

// 01 · Start here

Your first reverse shell.

If you've never popped a box, done a CTF, or used Kali Linux, start with these. TryHackMe first — it's the gentlest on-ramp and what I point every new student to. Once you're comfortable, expand out.

TryHackMe
Freemium
Free tier · Premium $14/mo · Beginner-friendly

Start here. Gamified rooms walk you through attacks and defense step-by-step. The free tier covers a lot — full learning paths like "Pre Security" and "Jr Penetration Tester" are excellent. Best on-ramp for students with zero hacking experience.

tryhackme.com
PortSwigger Web Security Academy
Free
100% free · Labs + theory · Web application focus

The best free resource for learning web application security — made by the company behind Burp Suite. SQL injection, XSS, SSRF, authentication bypass, deserialization — all covered with interactive labs. Essential if AppSec interests you.

portswigger.net/web-security
OverTheWire
Free
100% free · SSH-based wargames · Command line

The classic Linux / command-line wargame series. Start with Bandit — it teaches you terminal skills one puzzle at a time. If you can't navigate Linux comfortably, cybersecurity will be a constant struggle. Start fixing that here.

overthewire.org/wargames
PicoCTF
Free
100% free · CMU-maintained · Puzzle-based

Carnegie Mellon's free CTF platform. Perfect introduction to the CTF format with progressively harder challenges across crypto, forensics, web, binary exploitation, and more. Annual competition every spring — a great first CTF to enter.

picoctf.org
// 02 · Next level

Past beginner? Level up.

Once TryHackMe starts feeling easy, it's time to move to harder platforms. If you're going to pay for anything, HackTheBox VIP is the best value at $14/month for intermediate-level students. Commit to it seriously — an abandoned subscription is just a donation.

HackTheBox
Freemium
Free tier · VIP $14/mo · Intermediate–Advanced

The industry standard for hands-on offensive practice. More realistic and unforgiving than TryHackMe — closer to actual engagements. Free tier has active machines; VIP unlocks retired machines with official writeups, which is where real learning happens. Pair with IppSec's YouTube walkthroughs.

hackthebox.com
HackTheBox Academy
Freemium
Pay-per-module · Structured curriculum · Career paths

Separate from HTB's machine platform — this is their structured learning environment with career-path modules (Penetration Tester, Bug Bounty Hunter, SOC Analyst). More academic feel, great if you prefer guided learning over open-ended challenges.

academy.hackthebox.com
VulnHub
Free
100% free · Downloadable VMs · Self-hosted

Download vulnerable VMs and attack them in your own lab — completely free, no subscription. Great for practicing when offline or stress-testing your home lab setup. The "OSCP-like" list is famous for OSCP prep.

vulnhub.com
// 03 · Blue team & SOC

Defending, not attacking.

Most cybersecurity jobs are defensive — SOC analysts, incident responders, threat hunters. CyberDefenders and LetsDefend are both solid, with different strengths. Use both if you can.

CyberDefenders
Freemium
Free challenges · Paid Pro $39/mo · Blue team labs

Analyst-oriented. Scenario-based challenges where you investigate real artifacts — memory dumps, PCAPs, disk images, email headers. Free tier is genuinely useful, unlike many "freemium" platforms. Strong for building DFIR muscles.

cyberdefenders.org
LetsDefend
Freemium
Free tier · Paid $25/mo · SOC simulation

SOC simulator. Closer to the day-to-day experience of a tier-1 SOC analyst — ticket queues, alert triage, SIEM investigations. Great for showing the work in interviews because the platform looks like an actual SOC dashboard.

letsdefend.io
Blue Team Labs Online
Freemium
Free challenges · Paid $19/mo · Community-driven

Defender-focused CTFs covering investigations, threat hunting, and incident response. Challenges are contributed by the community, so quality varies — but the best ones are excellent practice. Complements CyberDefenders well.

blueteamlabs.online
Splunk Boss of the SOC
Free
Free · Splunk-specific · Realistic SIEM

Splunk's official SOC simulation. If you're targeting SOC roles, this is essential practice — it builds the Splunk query fluency that differentiates SOC candidates in hiring. Pair with the free Splunk Core Certified User cert.

bots.splunk.com
// 04 · CTF competitions

Prove it under pressure.

CTFs teach what certifications don't — how to think under pressure, solve problems creatively, collaborate under a deadline. Employers respect CTF participation because it's hard to fake. Add these to your résumé.

National Cyber League (NCL)
~$35
Twice a year · Spring / Fall seasons · Individual + team

The best CTF for college students. Structured challenges across domains students are actually hired for — forensics, cryptography, web exploitation, scanning. Get the NCL scouting report after competing — it's literally a résumé attachment showing employers which skills you demonstrated.

nationalcyberleague.org
NCCDC / CCDC
Free (team)
Spring season · Team defense · College-only

Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition — 8-person teams defend a network from professional red teams while keeping business services running. Most prestigious collegiate blue-team competition in the US. If your school has a team, join it.

nationalccdc.org
SANS Holiday Hack Challenge
Free
Annual · December–January · All skill levels

Free every year from SANS. A creative, story-driven CTF with Christmas/winter theming, challenges across all disciplines, and active walkthroughs afterward. Best annual CTF for learning at any level — take it seriously.

sans.org/holiday-hack-challenge
DEF CON CTF
Free (to watch)
Annually in August · Las Vegas · Expert level

The "world championship" of CTFs — you probably won't compete until senior-level, but attending DEF CON itself (badge hacking, villages, workshops) is formative. Community, talks, and smaller CTFs at the conference are accessible to all levels.

defcon.org
CTFtime
Free
Event tracker · Global calendar · Team rankings

Not a CTF itself — the global calendar of upcoming CTFs, team rankings, and past writeups. Bookmark it. Most weekends somewhere in the world, a CTF is running.

ctftime.org
BSides Houston
Low-cost
Annual · Houston, TX · Conference + CTF

Local to Houston students. BSides conferences are community-driven, low-cost ($20–50), and often have CTFs onsite. Best opportunity to meet local cyber professionals face-to-face. Volunteer if you can — free entry and deeper networking.

bsideshouston.org

// 06 · Home lab

Build your own playground.

Every serious cyber student should have a home lab. It's not optional. Free courses and online labs teach you the what; a home lab teaches you the how when things break — and they will break. This is where real practitioners separate from people who just pass exams.

The starter stack

You don't need thousands of dollars of equipment. A used desktop with 32GB RAM (~$300 on eBay or Craigslist) is enough to run everything below simultaneously. Start small, add as you go.

Hypervisor
Proxmox VE
Free, Debian-based. Simpler than ESXi for home use.
Firewall / router
pfSense or OPNsense
Free. Learn real networking with VLANs, IDS, VPN.
Domain controller
Windows Server 2022 Eval
Free 180-day evaluation. Practice AD attacks safely.
Attacker VM
Kali Linux or Parrot OS
Free. The standard pentester workstation.
Target VMs
Metasploitable, VulnHub VMs
Free. Intentionally vulnerable targets to practice against.
SIEM
Security Onion or Splunk Free
Both free. Build detection engineering muscle.
What to actually do with it

The lab is only valuable if you attack your own network. Specific projects that will teach you more than any class:

• Build an Active Directory domain with 2–3 Windows VMs, then try to compromise it using BloodHound + impacket.
• Set up Security Onion, generate attack traffic, and learn to detect it.
• Deploy a honeypot (T-Pot) on your router and observe real internet attacks hitting it.
• Build a small web app, find its vulnerabilities, patch them.
• Capture network traffic with Wireshark during different activities and learn to read it.

Document everything — your lab is your portfolio. A public GitHub repo or blog describing what you built beats a résumé line every time.

Cybersecurity runs on Linux. Automation runs on Python. You cannot be effective in this field without both. These are not optional.

Start here if you've never coded. The most accessible free Python course online. Audit for free on Coursera or use the pythonforeverybody.com materials directly. Finish this before trying security-specific Python.

py4e.com
Programming with Mosh
Freemium
Free YouTube · Paid courses · Beginner → Intermediate

World-class Python instruction. Mosh Hamedani's free 6-hour Python tutorial on YouTube is one of the best Python intros on the internet — clear explanations, well-paced, no fluff. His paid courses on codewithmosh.com go deeper if you want structured progression into Django, APIs, and data structures. Great complement to Py4e.

codewithmosh.com

100% free · Browser-based · Structured curriculum

Linux fundamentals from scratch — file systems, processes, permissions, shell scripting, networking. Work through this before touching Kali or trying CTFs or you'll constantly be fighting the OS instead of learning security.

linuxjourney.com

~$35 book
Justin Seitz · 2nd edition · Offensive Python

After you know basic Python, this book teaches you to write your own offensive tools — network scanners, keyloggers, trojans, web shell clients. Dated in some spots, but the fundamentals are evergreen.

nostarch.com/black-hat-python2E

// 08 · Python, Data & AI

AI is eating cybersecurity. Learn the language.

AI is already reshaping cyber work — SOC alert triage, threat intel, malware analysis, and phishing detection are all being automated or augmented. Students entering the field without Python, data, and AI literacy will be at a significant disadvantage. This section is the fastest path to that literacy.

Why this matters for cyber specifically

// Not optional. Not "nice-to-have." Foundational.

Three things are happening simultaneously. First, AI is automating entry-level cyber tasks — tier-1 SOC alert triage, log correlation, phishing email classification. Second, AI is creating new attack surfaces — prompt injection, model poisoning, data exfiltration via LLM queries, deepfake social engineering. Third, AI is becoming a force multiplier for defenders — analysts who can use AI tools effectively will outperform those who can't, dramatically.

You don't need to become a data scientist. You need enough Python + data fluency to: (a) understand what your AI tools are actually doing, (b) recognize when they're wrong, (c) customize or automate around them, and (d) defend systems that use them.

The goal of this section: practical, applied AI/data skills layered on top of the cybersecurity foundation. Not a PhD in machine learning.

Datasets & platforms

Kaggle
Free
Datasets + competitions + notebooks · Google-owned · Huge community

The default destination. Thousands of datasets (including cybersecurity ones like malware samples, phishing URLs, network intrusion logs), live competitions with prize money, and shared notebooks where you can learn from others' analyses. Create an account, download a dataset, and start poking at it.

kaggle.com
Kaggle Learn
Free
Micro-courses · Python · Pandas · Data viz · Intro ML

Kaggle's free micro-courses — each one a few hours, with hands-on notebooks. "Intro to Machine Learning," "Pandas," "Data Visualization." Ideal starting points for students who want the minimum viable data science exposure without committing to a semester-long course.

kaggle.com/learn
Google Colab
Free
Free cloud Python · GPU access · No install needed

Jupyter notebooks in the browser with free GPU access — no local setup required. This is how most courses and tutorials are now delivered. Can handle real machine learning workloads. The free tier is generous; the paid tier is optional for heavier workloads.

colab.research.google.com
Hugging Face
Free
Pretrained models · Datasets · Tutorials · GitHub of AI

The GitHub of AI. Hundreds of thousands of pretrained models you can download and run, datasets organized and searchable, and excellent free tutorials. If you want to use AI models (not build them from scratch), start here.

huggingface.co
CIC (Canadian Institute for Cybersecurity) Datasets
Free
Academic-grade · Intrusion detection · Malware · Network traffic

The most-used cybersecurity datasets in academic ML research. CIC-IDS2017 for intrusion detection, CIC-AndMal for Android malware, CICDDoS2019 for denial-of-service attacks. If you want to do a capstone or portfolio project with real cyber data, these are the starting points.

unb.ca/cic/datasets
Microsoft Security Datasets
Free
GitHub repo · Real-world security telemetry · Attack simulations

Microsoft-curated, open-sourced security datasets for detection engineering and ML research. Organized by MITRE ATT&CK technique — find attack data by the TTP you want to detect. Good bridge between security concepts and data analysis work.

github.com/OTRF/Security-Datasets
UCI Machine Learning Repository
Free
Classic datasets · Academic standard · 600+ datasets

The original, long-running repository of ML datasets used in academic research. Great for practicing fundamental ML techniques before moving to cyber-specific data. Iris, Titanic, wine quality — these are the "hello world" datasets every data person has seen.

archive.ics.uci.edu
Papers with Code
Free
Latest research · Runnable implementations · State-of-the-art

ML research papers paired with runnable code. Useful for staying current on what's actually new versus hype, and for seeing how cutting-edge techniques are actually implemented. The security section tracks papers on adversarial ML, malware detection, and intrusion detection.

paperswithcode.com/area/computer-security

// 09 · Free formal courses

University-grade, zero cost.

Top universities and federal programs have put their best cybersecurity coursework online for free. Worth integrating into your learning if you're serious.

SANS Cyber Aces Online
Free
SANS · Self-paced · Foundational

SANS gives away their foundational training for free. Three courses — Operating Systems, Networking, System Administration — the prerequisites they expect before paid SANS courses. High quality, high rigor. Start here for self-study.

tutorials.cyberaces.org
MIT OpenCourseWare
Free
MIT · Full course materials · Self-directed

Full MIT course materials free online — including 6.858 Computer Systems Security, the course that trained many top security researchers. Lecture videos, assignments, labs. University-grade material, zero cost, no login required.

ocw.mit.edu
CyberCorps: Scholarship for Service
Scholarship
Federal scholarship · Tuition + stipend · Service obligation

Federal scholarship for cybersecurity students. Covers tuition, fees, plus a substantial annual stipend ($27K undergrad / $37K grad). Service commitment: work for federal government for the same number of years you received funding. Life-changing if you qualify.

sfs.opm.gov
DoD Cyber Scholarship Program
Scholarship
DoD · Undergrad + grad · Service obligation

DoD equivalent to CyberCorps. Scholarship in exchange for civilian DoD employment after graduation. Specifically focused on CAE-designated schools — so if your school has CAE status, check this before paying tuition out of pocket.

dodcio.defense.gov/Cyber-Workforce/CySP
Open Security Training
Free
OST2 · Low-level security · Self-paced

Deep free courses on reverse engineering, assembly, exploit development, and hardware security. Rigorous, technical — for students going into exploit development, malware analysis, or low-level research. Not beginner material.

ost2.fyi
NIST NICE Framework
Free
NIST · Career roadmap · Skills mapping

Not a course — a framework for mapping cyber careers to actual skills. Use the NICE Workforce Framework to understand what work roles exist, what competencies each requires, and target your learning accordingly. Especially useful for federal applicants.

nist.gov/itl/applied-cybersecurity/nice
// 10 · College pathways

Formal education, done right.

A degree isn't required to break into cyber — but the right one accelerates you without draining your savings. Community colleges increasingly offer four-year bachelor's degrees at a fraction of traditional university tuition, and federally-designated CAE schools unlock scholarships like CyberCorps SFS and DoD CySP.

Lone Star College — Cybersecurity

// Local Houston · Community college · Accessible tuition

Lone Star College offers an accredited Bachelor of Applied Technology in Cybersecurity — one of a growing number of community colleges nationally that grant four-year degrees at community-college tuition rates. For Houston-area students, that's often thousands of dollars cheaper per year than the traditional four-year alternatives.

Smaller classes than UH or Texas A&M, faculty with real industry backgrounds, and a curriculum that maps to NIST NICE workforce categories. The BAT degree is specifically designed for students who want a bachelor's degree without the cost and structure of a traditional research university.

Degree or not: formal education gets you the foundation. Labs and home practice get you the skills. You need both — don't skip either.

lonestar.edu/cybersecurity →
